Get Rid of Moles in Your Garden Once and For All


Got moles? If you’ve got moles, chances are you want to eliminate them. Moles can ruin a perfect garden in no time flat. But how? How do you get rid of those pesky little critters?

There are a few different ways that you can go about getting rid of moles in your garden. You can trap them, poison them, or repel them. Each method has pros and cons, so choosing the proper plan for your needs is essential.

Trapping moles is perhaps the most humane method of getting rid of them.


There are a few different mole traps on the market, but the harpoon trap is the most common and effective. Harpoon traps work by driving a sharp metal rod into the ground, where the mole is active. When the mole tunnel underneath the harpoon trap, the metal rod pierces the mole, killing it instantly. While this method is effective, it takes time to set up the trap correctly.

If you’re looking for a more humane way to eliminate moles, you can try using a live trap. Live traps work by baiting the trap with food and capturing the mole alive when it enters the trap. Once captured, you can then release the mole into an area away from your home or property. While this method is more time-consuming, it is more humane to get rid of moles.

Poisoning moles is another option for getting rid of them.


Mole poisons come in two forms: granular baits and liquid pesticides. Granular baits are applied to the ground, where they are eaten by the moles. The poison then kills the moles from within. Liquid pesticides are used in tunnels and burrows, and when inhaled by the moles, they kill them quite quickly. If you have a mole problem, it is best to contact a professional for help. The third option for getting rid of moles is to repel them.


Mole repellents come in many forms, but ultrasonic devices and sonic spikes are the most common. Ultrasonic devices emit sound waves that are supposed to irritate moles and drive them away from your garden. Sonic spikes emit a high-pitched noise that also goes away from your garden. The downside to ultrasonic devices and sonic spikes is that they only provide temporary relief from moles; as soon as you turn off the device or remove the spikes, the moles will return.

Consider using a mole trap if you’re looking for a more permanent solution to your mole problem. Mole traps are designed to kill moles and are relatively easy to use. Just bait the trap with something moles like to eat (worms or grubs), set it in an active tunnel, and wait for the trap to do its job. Once the mole is caught in the trap, you can dispose of it however you see fit.

Mole traps are the most effective way to get rid of moles, but they do require a bit of patience and effort to use. If you’re not willing to put in the time to set and check mole traps, your best bet is to learn to live with the moles. After all, they’re not doing any harm; they’re just trying to find food and a place to live, like the rest of us.

Conclusion:


So there you have it! A few different methods for getting rid of those pesky moles once and for all. The key is to choose the right plan for your needs. If you’re looking for a quick fix, poisoning might be your best bet. But trapping or repelling could be better options if you’re looking for a more long-term solution. Whatever you choose, just make sure you take care to protect your pets and children from coming into contact with any poisonous substances!
